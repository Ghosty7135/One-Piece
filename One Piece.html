Enter the Grand Line

The legendary treasure, One Piece, is waiting for the one who can find it.

The Epic Tale

Gol D. Roger was known as the "Pirate King," the strongest and most infamous being to have sailed the Grand Line. The capture and execution of Roger by the World Government brought a change throughout the world. His last words before his death revealed the existence of the greatest treasure in the world, One Piece.

Monkey D. Luffy, a 17-year-old boy who defies your standard definition of a pirate, embarks on a journey to find the treasure and become the next Pirate King.

The World of One Piece

The world is mostly covered in ocean, with a single massive continent known as the Red Line. The most dangerous sea is the Grand Line, where the weather is unpredictable and islands are home to bizarre phenomena and powerful enemies.

Mysterious Powers

  • Devil Fruits: Mysterious fruits that grant incredible powers in exchange for the ability to swim. They fall into three categories: Paramecia (body alterations), Zoan (animal transformations), and Logia (elemental control).
  • Haki: A mysterious power that allows users to harness their spiritual energy. It manifests as Observation (prediction), Armament (invisible armor), and the rare Conqueror's Haki (overpowering the will of others).

The Four Emperors (Yonko)

The Yonko are the four most powerful pirate captains in the world, residing in the second half of the Grand Line, known as the New World. They are so powerful that they rule over territories like kings.

  • Shanks "The Red-Haired": The only member of the original Yonko remaining, known for his incredible Haki and leadership of the Red Hair Pirates.
  • Marshall D. Teach "Blackbeard": The only man to possess two Devil Fruit powers simultaneously (Tremor-Tremor and Dark-Dark fruits).
  • Monkey D. Luffy "Straw Hat": Newly crowned after the events in Wano, representing the "New Era" and the freedom of the seas.
  • Buggy "The Genius Jester": The unexpected leader of the Cross Guild, which has issued bounties on Marines.

Legendary predecessors who once held this title include Whitebeard (Edward Newgate), Kaido (The King of Beasts), and Big Mom (Charlotte Linlin).
